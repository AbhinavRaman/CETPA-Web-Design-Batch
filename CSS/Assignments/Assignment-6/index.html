ANDES

Andes Mountains in South America is the longest mountain range in the world. About 7,000 km (4,350 miles) long, stretching from Venezuela to Argentina and Chile.

Rocky Mountains

Around 4,800 km (3,000 miles) long, running from Canada to the southwestern United States.

The Great Dividing Range

About 3,500 km (2,175 miles) long, extending along the eastern coast of Australia.

Transantarctic Mountains

Roughly 3,500 km (2,175 miles) long, dividing East and West Antarctica.

Himalayas

Approximately 2,400 km (1,500 miles) long, stretching across Nepal, India, Bhutan, China, and Pakistan.
